Paper Description

In modern supply chain management, visualizing and analyzing food cold chain data is crucial for assessing and optimizing food safety. However, visualization expertise and efficient data access pose challenges for analysts. To address these issues, we propose VIS-Llama, an automated visualization generation model based on large language models (LLMs). VIS-Llama leverages the Llama2 model optimized with Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) technology, trained on a dataset of 131,910 pairs from the Food Cold Chain Corpus to generate high-quality Vega-Lite visualizations. We evaluate VIS-Llama through quantitative and qualitative experiments, demonstrating its effectiveness and practicality. Our web application provides convenient exploration services for cold chain analysts. Our contributions include the construction of the Food Cold Chain Corpus, development and optimization of VIS-Llama, and comprehensive evaluation through experiments and user studies. This work highlights the potential of LLMs for domain-specific data visualization and learning complex strategies.


How It Works


Our workflow outlines the path from constructing the food cold chain corpus to developing and evaluating the VIS-Llama model and integrating the visualization application.
(a) Food Cold Chain Corpus Construction: Collect and integrate food cold chain data from various sources, clean the data, and build the comprehensive Food Cold Chain Corpus.
(b) VIS-Llama Model Development: Improve the quality of input data through feature processing, and then optimize the Llama2 model using LoRA technology with training data pairs extracted from the corpus to automatically generate visual content.
(c) Model Evaluation: Perform a comprehensive evaluation of the VIS-Llama model, assessing both text generation and visualization quality to confirm its applicability and reliability in the food cold chain domain.
(d) Visualization Application: Develop a web application based on the VIS-Llama model that enables users to generate the desired visualizations. Conduct visualization examples and user studies to assess the model's effectiveness.
